Water birds

Many bird species thrive in or near water. These birds have adapted to environments like lakes, rivers, wetlands, and coastlines. Whether they are swimming, diving, or hunting for food in water, these birds have specialized features such as webbed feet or waterproof feathers to help them live comfortably in these habitats. They play an important role in maintaining healthy ecosystems, controlling pests, and ensuring the balance of aquatic life.

Great Blue Heron

The Great Blue Heron is a large wading bird found in North America, often seen standing still in shallow water, waiting to catch fish. Common Kingfisher

The Common Kingfisher is a small but vibrant bird often found along rivers, streams, and lakes. With its brilliant blue and orange plumage, it hunts for fish by diving into the water at impressive speeds. These birds are excellent swimmers and can stay underwater for short periods to catch their prey.

Mallard Duck

The Mallard Duck is one of the most well-known waterfowl, commonly seen in ponds, lakes, and rivers across the world. Mallards are recognized by their bright green head (for males) and brown feathers (for females). They are highly adaptable and can live in both freshwater and brackish water, feeding on aquatic plants and small invertebrates.
