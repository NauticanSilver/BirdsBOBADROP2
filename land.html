Land Birds

Land birds thrive in various terrestrial habitats like forests, grasslands, and urban areas. These birds include the resourceful pigeons and the charismatic cockatoos, each with their unique behaviors and adaptations. They play vital roles in controlling pests, pollinating plants, and dispersing seeds, enriching our ecosystems and lives.

Pigeon

Known for their adaptability and resourcefulness, pigeons are common in urban areas worldwide. They have a remarkable homing ability and have been used throughout history to carry messages over long distances.

Cockatoo

These charismatic birds are native to Australia and the surrounding islands. With their striking crests and intelligent behavior, cockatoos are known for their loud calls and playful antics. Popular species include the Sulphur-crested Cockatoo and the Galah.

Bald Eagle

A symbol of strength and freedom, the Bald Eagle is native to North America. Although they are often associated with water due to their diet of fish, they primarily nest in large trees in forested areas. Bald Eagles are known for their powerful build and distinctive white head and tail.
